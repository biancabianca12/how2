When  the  first  European  explorers came to the 
New World, full of the pompous assertion that  if 
they  had  not  seen it,  it  was new, they began to 
make  maps  of the land and  to give names to the 
places  they  were  charting.  Sometimes,  through 
contact  with   Native   Americans,  an  anglicized 
version  of  a  particular  tribe's  name  for a place  
would    be   kept.    Cheektowaga.   Tonawanda. 
These  areas are now the white suburbs, tentacles 
sprawling  out   from   the   shopping   malls.  The 
Europeans had  little  understanding  of the Native 
American's     experience   of    land   and    place. 
Pronunciation  is  not   equal  to  an understanding. 
A  name  does  and  does  not  maintain  a  culture.
