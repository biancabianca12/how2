The Four Museums are the Four Mirrors.12 In the film Four Corners there are four paintings reflecting at each other. Four Paintings structuring an eighty minute film. Four paintings make four corners make a room. And in-between, landscape. It has been three weeks or so since viewing the film. Detail is becoming less distinct. There is no end of detail that is without significance.13 The first painting is a Cezanne landscape; the second an African American man by a painter named Moses; the third a wall painting at four corners. The voice-over says the artist who painted the wall was a woman, it states this as a fact, yet the painting is from a time when history was not recorded with names. The fourth painting, the final corner, is a flag painting by Jasper Johns. These inaccessible surfaces deny any definite meaning in the most definite way.14 The camera, always static, photographs landscape from the southwest, mostly desert, some urban scapes, and fills the space between the four corners. Four Corners which rest on a flat surface - the film screen. We build our own walls. The viewer can only experience distance.

I'm sure of a distance from it, and I'm all for fabricating as much distance as possible.15

One is not allowed into the postcard landscape, one is constantly aware of oneself as the viewer, forced to be making connections between what is seen and heard. The mistake in it is to have believed that the reflection of nature is nature.16 This is a film about identity; James Benning made the film about himself. An American self surrounded by degenerating city scapes and an unapproachable landscape. The west is still wild.
