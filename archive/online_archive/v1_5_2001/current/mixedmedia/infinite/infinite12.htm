It is easy to imagine the scene, in the epic historical dramatic film version of Mina Loy's life, where Arthur Cravan sails away never to be seen again. It is easy to imagine how wrong it will be. The blurb on the back of the biography of Mina Loy says "Loy's life is the stuff of Hollywood legend: it would (and probably will!) make a great movie . . ." Rich colors, a fantastic opportunity for a costume designer and an art director. Recreating Mina's clothes. Her self-decorated apartments. Those lamp shades.

Why a picture's worth a thousand words! Until you get to the gates of The English Department where a picture proves your inability to write.
