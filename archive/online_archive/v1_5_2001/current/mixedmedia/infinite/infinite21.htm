If a tree falls in the middle of a forest, and nobody hears it, does it make a sound?

She swings her house door open to the shouts of a couple's argument. Who, he asks, who else have I been spending my time with since I got out of jail? Each of his words is a performance. He yells so that all the street can hear his pronunciations. Her responses are inaudible but her body does not spell fear. Priorities, he shouts, I know my priorities. And she, the witness, shuts her car door and drives away leaving the scene to play out but to remain unfinished for her. This happened this morning.

 

This morning was a week ago. This morning was a week ago and seven seconds. This morning was a week ago and seventeen seconds. There was a this morning this morning but it was a different this morning. Some this mornings are frozen into an image which can be revisited but which will, on each occasion, be viewed differently.
