Four Corners is a film by James Benning, a film about history, and some of the stuff is made up. A film about looking. Being conscious of the act of looking. Four corners where four states meet. Four Paintings are the film's frame. All Static shots. 40 seconds long. Each titled segment has the same exact amount of words. This is structuralist filmmaking. It begins with a shot of a crackling fire. The place stories are told around. Not where they began but where they begin for those that tell and those that listen. Cut to a Cezanne painting. Stare at it so long that one begins to wonder if it is painting. The voice-over is dry. The story of a European. Of Europe's conquest of this Nuevo Mundo
