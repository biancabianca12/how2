A map, like all forms of documentation, creates its own reality as well as marking the location of a place. When we name it , life exists.32 Memory can be seen as an enlarged inset on a map drawn with an erasable pencil. Events are distinct but their placement can shift. 

      Imagination is not to avoid reality - 33

She remembers a family trip at about the age of seven. They were driving through Northern California and she insisted upon stopping at all the graveyards they passed. Her family indulged her, but after the first one, would not enter with her. She can remember closing the car door and feeling scared to go walk among the dead by herself. But also an urgency, an inability to back out. So she ran up the small grassy hill and carefully walked upon the margins of the graves, trying to imagine the borders of the coffins, so as not to walk directly upon the dead. This, she was sure, was a form of politeness. She cannot remember if she visited any more graveyards that day. She has, at times, been accused of an excessive morbidity.
