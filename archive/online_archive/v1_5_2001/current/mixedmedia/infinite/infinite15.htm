When Robert Smithson was a little boy growing up in Passaic, New Jersey his Doctor was William Carlos Williams. When he was sick his parents took him to the Doctor who wrote but there was no talk of writing. Only of pains in the belly, and if it hurt here but not there. Later Smithson would read the Doctor and his own writing would make a trail back to the words of the Doctor.

Language is built, not written. 7
There can only be pyramids of words, tombs. 8
Usage preceeds meaning. 9
To progress from word to word is to suck a nipple. 10

When we are ill a Doctor may cure us. Science can come to our aid providing us with the penicillin to kill the attacking bacteria. Where does the Doctor , trained in science, turn. He looks to art. An art based in the imagination. He knows this to be more powerful than the science which employs him.

He is a doctor. He loves bare facts. He is also a poet, he must recreate everything to suit himself. How can he reconcile these two selves? 11
