“...frozen posture and resemblance of organism to its environment (or part of it) to avoid detection; used by both predators and prey" [Biol.] My syntax will understand this as repetition, the sacred space of the tautologous sentence. "A sudden suspension of sensation and volition, the body and limbs preserving the position that may be given them, while the action of the heart and lungs continues.” [Med.] Aporia, mistaken as abjection. The near-death of adoration, given. A mode of attention – which mimics love – compulsive and helpless, rapt, which sees nothing and wishes itself, impossibly large, predator and prey.
