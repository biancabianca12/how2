Editor's Notes

"When knowledge in the body starts to cross over" (Mei-mei Berssenbrugge's line from Kate's Talk, p. 10) suggests, among many things, the tension of approaching and breaking and entering the new. Claiming that which has been prohibited by previously established category, æsthetic demarcation. Negotiation with something only apprehended. That moment when the unsaid may finally speak. . . .Thus the necessity of crossover between such isolated identities as body and video, movement and word, poem and prose, "creative" work and "critical" work.
